How We Collect and Use Your Personal Information

Personal information refers to various information recorded electronically or otherwise that can identify a specific natural person or reflect their activities, either alone or in combination with other data.

In compliance with applicable laws and regulations, and in accordance with the principles of lawfulness, legitimacy, and necessity, we may collect and use your personal information during your use of our services, including but not limited to your name, email address, phone number, device ID, and location information. All information you provide is submitted by yourself upon registration or obtained from our third-party partners.

How We Store and Protect Your Personal Information

As a general rule, we retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, such as maintaining your account to access our services.

We protect your personal information using industry-standard security measures and encrypt critical data to prevent unauthorized access, disclosure, use, modification, damage, or loss. We use encryption technologies to ensure data confidentiality and trusted protection mechanisms against malicious attacks.

To further strengthen privacy protection, we anonymize data upon collection. Even in our own databases, we do not store associated or plain-text private data.

We use your personal information lawfully and appropriately as needed for the management of our daily business operations and to better serve our legitimate interests. We only use such data internally and do not share it with any third parties.

We may share your personal information as required by laws, regulations, or mandatory government authorities. When receiving such disclosure requests, we will require corresponding legal documents, such as subpoenas or investigation orders. We believe in being as transparent as possible within the scope permitted by law.

We may share, transfer, or publicly disclose your personal information without your prior consent in the following circumstances:
